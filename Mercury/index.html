Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. With virtually no atmosphere to retain heat, it experiences the greatest temperature variations of any planet, from scorching hot to freezing cold.

Diameter

4,879 km (0.38 Earths)

Temperature

430°C (day) / -180°C (night)

Composition

Large iron core, thin silicate mantle

Length of Day

176 Earth Days

Scientific Discoveries

  1. Closest planet to the Sun at 57.9 million km average distance.
  2. Has a very thin atmosphere, called an exosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, and hydrogen.
  3. Experiences extreme temperature swings between day and night, the largest in the solar system.
  4. Completes an orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days, the fastest of all planets.
  5. Its surface is heavily cratered, similar to our Moon, indicating a long history of impacts.
  6. Despite its proximity to the Sun, water ice exists in permanently shadowed craters at its poles.
