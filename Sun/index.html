G-type Star

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, heated to incandescence by nuclear fusion reactions in its core.

Diameter

1.39 million km (109 Earths)

Surface Temperature

5,500°C

Age

~4.6 Billion Years

Mass

333,000 Earths

Scientific Discoveries

  1. The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the mass in the solar system.
  2. Light from the Sun takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  3. The Sun's core is so hot (15 million °C) that nuclear fusion occurs, converting hydrogen to helium.
  4. Eventually, in about 5 billion years, the Sun will expand to become a red giant, engulfing the inner planets.
  5. The Sun has an 11-year cycle of solar activity, including sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections.
  6. The Sun's outer atmosphere, the corona, is paradoxically much hotter than its visible surface, reaching up to 2 million °C.
